## Post 1 — LinkedIn (Monday)

**Title**: What If MBTI Existed for How You Code with AI? — Introducing 5 AI Coding Personalities

**Summary**:
Just as 16Personalities created a billion-test phenomenon by asking "what type are you?", NoMoreAISlop brings that same question to AI coding: "What's your AI collaboration style?" The twist — it's not a self-assessment quiz. It analyzes your actual session data.

**Body Structure**:

1. **Opening Hook**: "What if MBTI existed for how you use AI?"
   - 16Personalities: 1B+ tests completed, the world's most popular personality test
   - DZone Developer Personality Quiz: showed developers love typing themselves
   - But NO personality test exists for "how you work with AI"

2. **The 5 AI Coding Styles**:

   🏗️ **Architect**: Plans before prompting. Gives AI clear specs, constraints, and examples. Thinks in systems, not snippets. "A codebase without design is a building without blueprints."

   🔬 **Scientist**: Verifies everything. Forms hypotheses, tests AI output, checks evidence. Slower but more accurate. "I don't trust AI's answer. I verify it."

   🤝 **Collaborator**: Works through dialogue. Uses iterative feedback loops to progressively improve output. "Coding is pair programming with AI."

   ⚡ **Speedrunner**: Moves fast. Accepts most AI suggestions and prioritizes execution speed above all. "Perfect is the enemy of shipped. Fix it later."

   🔧 **Craftsman**: Obsesses over code quality. Refines AI output to meet personal standards — naming, structure, readability. "My name is on this code."

3. **The Twist**: "This classification doesn't come from self-diagnosis."
   - Self-assessment is always biased (METR study: devs think they're 24% faster, but they're 19% slower)
   - NoMoreAISlop analyzes actual Claude Code / Cursor conversation logs
   - The gap between perceived and actual style is often surprising

4. **The 3 Control Levels**: Each style also has a control dimension
   - Explorer (0-34): Gives AI significant freedom
   - Navigator (35-64): Balances AI autonomy with human direction
   - Cartographer (65-100): Maintains tight strategic control

## Post 4 — LinkedIn (Thursday)

**Title**: "I'm Great at Using AI" — Are You Sure? Data Says Otherwise

**Summary**:
Highlight the gap between self-perception and reality in AI tool usage. Draw on the Dunning-Kruger effect and METR's perception bias data to argue that data-driven assessment is the only reliable way to understand your AI collaboration patterns.

**Body Structure**:

1. **Opening**: The Dunning-Kruger effect meets AI coding
   - Most developers rate themselves as "above average" at using AI tools
   - But METR's data shows a 43-percentage-point gap between perceived and actual performance
   - If we can't even accurately judge whether AI makes us faster or slower, how can we judge the quality of our AI collaboration?

2. **The Self-Assessment Problem**:
   - Self-reported surveys: subjective, socially desirable answers
   - Manager observation: not scalable, limited visibility into AI interactions
   - Code review metrics: only measures output, not the collaboration process
   - None of these capture HOW you interact with AI

3. **What Data-Driven Analysis Reveals**:
   - Developers who think they "carefully verify" often show acceptance rates above 80%
   - Developers who think they "move fast" sometimes spend more time on context engineering than they realize (which is actually a strength)
   - The gap between identity and behavior is the most interesting insight

4. **The NoMoreAISlop Approach**:
   - Analyzes actual session logs — not what you say, but what you do
   - 6-dimension scoring with evidence quotes from your real conversations
   - Personalized growth recommendations based on your actual patterns
