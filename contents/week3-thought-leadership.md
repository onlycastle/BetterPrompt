Prompt Engineering is dead. Context Engineering killed it.

Karpathy, June 2025: "Context engineering is the delicate art and science of filling the context window with just the right information for the next step."

It's not about clever prompts. It's about what the model SEES.

Think of it this way. Prompt Engineering is writing a good email to your coworker. Context Engineering is making sure your coworker has the right docs, codebase context, and project history BEFORE you ask.

The 5 pillars of Context Engineering:
📋 Selection: what info to include
📦 Compression: how to condense it
📐 Ordering: what sequence matters
🔒 Isolation: keeping contexts separate
📝 Format: structuring for the model
(ref: Martin Fowler's guide)

This is why @NoMoreAISlop measures Context Engineering as one of 6 dimensions: AI Collaboration Mastery, Context Engineering, Tool Mastery, Burnout Risk, AI Control Index, and Skill Resilience.

The best devs we've analyzed don't write better prompts. They engineer better contexts. CLAUDE.md files with project conventions. Rules files per directory. Structured task breakdowns. Progressive context building.

The 6 Dimensions of AI Coding Capability: Where Are You Strong, and Where Are You Weak?

GitHub's SPACE framework measured developer productivity across five dimensions: Satisfaction, Performance, Activity, Communication, and Efficiency. It was groundbreaking for its time.

But in 2026, the question has fundamentally shifted. It's no longer "how fast do you write code?" It's "how well do you work WITH AI?"

We need a new framework. Here are the 6 dimensions we've identified through analyzing thousands of developer-AI collaboration sessions:

1. AI Collaboration Mastery
What it measures: the quality of context you provide to AI.
Why it matters: input quality determines output quality. Garbage context in, garbage code out.

2. Context Engineering
What it measures: your WRITE-SELECT-COMPRESS-ISOLATE strategies.
Why it matters: this is the defining skill of 2026, as Karpathy has articulated.

3. Burnout Risk
What it measures: work pattern sustainability indicators.
Why it matters: sustainable AI usage vs the burnout spiral that 65% of developers are experiencing.

4. Tool Mastery
What it measures: effectiveness across different AI tools.
Why it matters: understanding tool-specific best practices and knowing when to use which tool.

5. AI Control Index
What it measures: strategic control vs vibe coding.
Why it matters: the difference between proactive orchestration and passive acceptance.

6. Skill Resilience
What it measures: independent coding ability retention.
Why it matters: can you still build without AI?

Let me go deeper on Skill Resilience, because I believe it's the most critical dimension for the AI era.

Luciano Nooijen, an engineer at Companion Group, shared his experience in MIT Technology Review. He used AI tools heavily at work. Then he started a side project without access to those tools, and found himself struggling with tasks that previously came naturally. "Things that used to be instinct became manual, sometimes even cumbersome," he said. His conclusion: the only way to maintain coding instinct is to regularly practice the grunt work, just as athletes still perform basic drills.

Stanford's research adds urgency: employment of developers aged 22 to 25 fell nearly 20% between 2022 and 2025. Foundational skills matter more than ever.
