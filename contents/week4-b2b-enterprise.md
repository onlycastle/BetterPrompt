## Post 1 — LinkedIn (Monday)

**Title**: 65% of Developers Experience Burnout — Is AI the Solution or the Cause?

**Summary**:
Present data showing that AI tools were expected to reduce burnout but are actually making it worse. Argue that the root cause isn't "AI tools" but "how teams use AI," and that making AI collaboration patterns visible at the team level can catch burnout risks early.

**Body Structure**:

1. **Opening**: The shocking data
   - 65% of developers experience burnout, even at organizations that adopted AI tools (2024 survey, 600+ engineers)
   - LeadDev 2025 survey of 617 engineering leaders: 22% critical burnout, 24% moderate
   - Stack Overflow 2025: 80% of developers are unhappy
   - 68% of tech workers report burnout symptoms, up from 49% just three years prior

2. **Three Mechanisms by Which AI Worsens Burnout**:

   a) **The Dopamine Trap**: AI delivers instant results → addiction to quick wins → avoidance of deep thinking → quality drops → more debugging → burnout
   - Reference: SUCCESS magazine on "The Dopamine Trap — the Rarely Discussed Source of Burnout"

   b) **Verification Burden Shift**: AI generates code, but the verification/debugging burden concentrates on senior engineers
   - 68% of developers say they spend MORE time fixing security issues in AI-generated code
   - arXiv study: AI-assisted code increases technical debt and maintenance burden

   c) **Replacement Anxiety**: Constant worry about "will AI replace me?"
   - Stanford study: 22-25 year old developer employment dropped 20%
   - This anxiety paradoxically drives overuse and burnout

3. **The Solution**: Not "use more AI" but "use AI better"
   - Team-level visibility into AI collaboration patterns enables early detection of burnout risk
   - NoMoreAISlop's Burnout Risk dimension: analyzes work hours, session patterns, late-night work frequency
   - Individual → Team → Organization assessment with data-driven diagnostics
