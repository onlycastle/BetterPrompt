## Post Option A: "The Honest Confession"

**Strategy**: 자신의 경험을 솔직하게 공유 → "나도 그래!" 반응 유도. LinkedIn에서 실패/어려움 공유가 성공 스토리보다 2~3배 더 높은 engagement를 얻음.

---

I spent the last few weeks watching non-developers try to build apps with AI.

What I saw was honestly heartbreaking.

The pattern is almost always the same:

→ Hour 1: "This is MAGIC. I built a login page in 5 minutes!"
→ Hour 3: "Why won't this button save to the database?"
→ Hour 8: "The AI says it fixed it. It didn't. Again."
→ Day 3: "I've burned $200 in credits fixing the same bug."
→ Week 2: Silence. Project abandoned.

The vibe coding promise was "anyone can build an app."

The reality? 90% of vibe-coded projects never make it to production.

And the people who struggle the most aren't talking about it.

They're the silent majority — feeling defeated, thinking they're just "not smart enough."

But the real problem isn't them. It's that nobody taught them HOW to work with AI effectively.

We know how to prompt. We know what to expect. We know when to stop and debug manually.

They don't. And no tool is teaching them.

I'm building something to change that. But first, I want to hear from you:

What's the #1 thing you wish someone had told you before you started using AI coding tools?

Drop it in the comments. Every answer helps.

#VibeCoding #AI #BuildInPublic #NonDeveloper #AITools

---

**Why this works**:
- Hook이 강함 — "heartbreaking"이라는 감정적 단어로 시작
- 타임라인 형태가 스크롤을 유도
- "silent majority" 표현이 공감 트리거
- 마지막 질문이 매우 구체적이고 답하기 쉬움
- "I'm building something"이 호기심 유발

**Expected comments to harvest**:
- 구체적인 실패 경험담
- "나도 똑같은 경험 했다"
- 비개발자들이 원하는 솔루션 힌트
- AI 도구별 구체적 불만

## Post Option B: "The Data Story"

**Strategy**: 개인 경험 + 데이터를 조합. 전문가 포지셔닝을 유지하면서도 진솔한 톤.

---

I've been researching how non-developers use AI coding tools.

One number stopped me cold:

86% of engineering leaders can't prove their AI tools are actually working.

Think about that. Companies are spending $500–$3,000 per developer per year on AI tools. And most of them have no idea if it's helping or hurting.

But here's what really got me — it's not just companies.

I talked to a designer who spent 3 days trying to connect a form to a database in Lovable. The AI kept saying "fixed!" but nothing changed. She burned through her entire month's credits in one debugging session.

I talked to a PM who built a beautiful prototype in Replit, then realized he had no idea how to deploy it. His "app" only existed on his laptop.

I talked to a founder who shipped a vibe-coded MVP — with API keys exposed in the client-side code. Someone scraped them. He had to beg OpenAI to forgive his bill.

The pattern is clear: AI makes Day 1 incredibly fast. But Day 2 — maintenance, security, debugging — is where everything falls apart.

And nobody is solving Day 2 for non-developers.

Curious: If you've used AI to build something without a traditional dev background — what was your "Day 2 moment"? The point where the magic stopped and reality hit?

#AI #VibeCoding #DeveloperExperience #BuildInPublic

---

**Why this works**:
- 숫자로 시작 → 신뢰감 구축
- 3개의 구체적 사례가 다양한 persona를 커버 (디자이너, PM, 창업자)
- "Day 2 moment"라는 명확한 프레임 제공 → 답변하기 쉬움
- 기업/개인 양쪽 모두에게 관련성

## Post Option C: "The Controversial Take"

**Strategy**: 약간의 논쟁성으로 engagement를 극대화. LinkedIn에서 의견이 갈리는 게시물이 가장 높은 도달률.

---

Hot take: "Vibe coding" has failed non-developers.

Not because the AI is bad. But because we told them they didn't need to learn anything.

"No coding required!"
"Build an app in 20 minutes!"
"Just describe what you want!"

We created millions of people who can START a project but can't FINISH one.

They can generate a login page but can't connect it to a database.
They can build a UI but can't deploy it.
They can create features but can't fix the bugs AI introduces.

And when they fail — which 90% do — they blame themselves. "I'm just not technical enough."

No. You were just never given the right skills.

The missing piece isn't better AI. It's better education on how to WORK WITH AI.

Not "prompt engineering tips." Not another YouTube tutorial.

A real framework for collaborating with AI effectively — understanding what to ask, when to stop, how to verify, and where AI needs human judgment.

This is what I'm working on. And I'd love your input:

What skill would make the biggest difference for someone learning to build with AI?

Share your thought below — especially if you've been through the struggle yourself.

#VibeCoding #AIEducation #NoCode #BuildInPublic #FutureOfWork

---

**Why this works**:
- "Hot take"로 시작 → 즉시 주목
- 업계를 비판하는 포지션 = 차별화
- "they blame themselves" → 강한 감정적 공감
- 솔루션 힌트를 자연스럽게 삽입
- 질문이 "skill" 하나에 집중 → 답변이 구체적으로 나올 가능성 높음

## Posting Strategy

### 추천 순서

| 순서 | Post | 타이밍 | 목적 |
|------|------|--------|------|
| 1차 | **Option D** (Simple Question) | 화~목 오전 8~10시 | 초기 engagement 테스트, 댓글 수집 |
| 2차 | **Option A** (Honest Confession) | 1주 후 | 스토리텔링으로 더 깊은 공감 유도 |
| 3차 | **Option B** (Data Story) | 2주 후 | 데이터 + 사례로 전문가 포지셔닝 |
| 4차 | **Option C** (Controversial Take) | 3주 후 | 논쟁으로 도달률 극대화 |

### 댓글 관리 전략

1. **모든 댓글에 1시간 이내 답변** — LinkedIn 알고리즘이 초기 engagement를 보상
2. **후속 질문으로 더 깊이 파기** — "That's fascinating. What tool were you using? What specifically broke?"
3. **DM 유도** — 특히 상세한 경험을 공유한 사람에게 "Would love to hear more — mind if I DM?"
4. **데이터 수집** — 모든 댓글을 스프레드시트에 기록 (문제 유형, 도구, persona, 감정)

### 댓글에서 수집할 데이터 포인트

| 카테고리 | 수집할 정보 |
|---------|-----------|
| **Who** | 직업, 기술 수준, 사용 도구 |
| **Problem** | 구체적으로 어디서 막혔는지 |
| **Emotion** | 좌절, 분노, 체념, 호기심 |
| **Current behavior** | 포기? 프리랜서 고용? 독학? |
| **Desired solution** | "이런 게 있었으면 좋겠다" |
| **WTP signal** | 돈을 쓴 경험, 비용에 대한 언급 |

## Engagement Boost Tips

- **링크를 본문에 넣지 마세요** — LinkedIn은 외부 링크가 있는 게시물의 도달률을 50% 감소시킴. 링크는 첫 번째 댓글에
- **해시태그 3~5개** — 5개 초과 시 스팸 판정

- **화~목 오전 8~10시 (타겟 시간대)** 가 최적 게시 시간
- **게시 후 1시간 이내 첫 댓글이 핵심** — 자신이 먼저 댓글을 달아 대화를 시작하세요
